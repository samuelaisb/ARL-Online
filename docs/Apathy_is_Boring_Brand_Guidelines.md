## 1. Brand Purpose, Vision & Tone

### 1.1 Mission & Core Goal
Our primary goal is to support, educate, and motivate youth across Canada to become active, contributing members of our democracy. We operate under the core philosophy that civic involvement is far more expansive than just voting every few years. It is about setting up youth to find their unique "lane" for long-term engagement and, ultimately, empowering them to take charge of the conditions and communities they live in.

### 1.2 Vision Statement
A Canada where every young Canadian is an active citizen, and where youth are robustly engaged in all aspects of the democratic process.

### 1.3 Brand Personality & Tone
The brand voice is **bold, bright, powerful, energetic, and exciting**. It leverages a dynamic personality designed to capture attention and shake off the stale, clinical reputation of traditional political or civic institutions. Because "Apathy is Boring," our communication must reflect the opposite: it must be engaging, accessible, youth-led, non-partisan, and action-oriented.

## 2. Logo & Wordmark Guidelines

To preserve the visual integrity, authority, and recognition of the brand, the logo and wordmarks must always be rendered exactly as specified below.

### 2.1 Logo Variants
The brand features bilingual and flexible variants to suit different mediums and layouts:

* **English Wordmarks:**
    * *Primary [Full]:* A heavy, block-stacked layout where "APATHY" sits directly above "IS BORING", creating a compact visual unit.
    * *Secondary [Full]:* A streamlined, horizontal one-line execution: `APATHY IS BORING`.
* **French Wordmarks:**
    * *Primary [Full]:* The block-stacked layout for French communication: "L'APATHIE" sits directly above "C'EST PLATE".
    * *Secondary [Full]:* The horizontal one-line execution: `L'APATHIE C'EST PLATE`.
* **Logo-Icon (The Identity Token):**
    * The short-form mixed-case abbreviation **AisB** (English) and **L'ACP** (French), typically featured inside a solid colored rectangle or circular badge to create a high-contrast anchor point for social assets.

### 2.2 Clear Space Requirements
For accessibility, clarity, and visual impact, a standard clear space boundary must be maintained around the logo at all times. No other graphic elements, text, or page borders may encroach upon this zone.
* **The Clear Space Rule:** For all wordmarks and icons, the minimum clear space on all sides must be exactly equal to the vertical height of the word **"IS"** (or the corresponding scale factor in the French variant).

### 2.3 Minimum Size Constraints
To guarantee readability and optimal visual performance across digital screen resolutions and physical print media, the logos must never appear smaller than the following thresholds:
* **Primary Stacked Logos:**
    * *Digital:* Minimum height of `30px`
    * *Print:* Minimum height of `8mm`
* **Secondary One-Line Logos:**
    * *Digital:* Minimum height of `20px`
    * *Print:* Minimum height of `5mm`

### 2.4 Strict Logo Violations ("Don'ts")
Any modification of the logo dilutes the brand identity. Avoid these specific misuses:
1.  **Distortion:** Do not squash, stretch, skew, or distort the proportions of the logo in any way.
2.  **Resolution:** Never reproduce or export the logo at a low resolution or in a pixelated format.
3.  **Orientation:** Do not rotate, tilt, angle, or flip the logo. It must always sit completely flat on the horizontal axis.
4.  **Altering Characters:** Do not modify individual letters, kerning, tracking, line-spacing, or character proportions.
5.  **Rearranging Elements:** Do not reposition, shift, or scale individual components or words independently.
6.  **Effects & Opacity:** Do not adjust transparency/opacity layers or add drop shadows, outer glows, gradients, or 3D effects.
7.  **Unauthorized Color Shifts:** Do not swap the official brand colors or alternate colors within the letters of the logo itself.
8.  **Low Contrast:** Never superimpose the logo onto a background color or busy imagery that restricts readability or creates low visual contrast.

### 3.2 Color Combinations & Contrast Standards
To satisfy WCAG AA accessibility guidelines for both small and large typography, our palette is mathematically divided into **Light/Bright Backgrounds (Group A)** and **Dark/Heavy Tones (Group B)**.

* **Group A (Light / Vivid / High-Reflectance Tones):** `Light`, `Lemon`, `Lavender`, `Pear`
* **Group B (Dark / Dense / High-Absorption Tones):** `Dark`, `Plum`, `Mint`, `Grape`

*Design Rule:* **Never** overlay Group A colors onto other Group A colors (e.g., Lemon text on a Lavender background is strictly prohibited). **Never** overlay Group B colors onto other Group B colors (e.g., Mint text on a Grape background is strictly prohibited). *Exception Note:* Avoid using tertiary colors (`Pear` and `Grape`) as background treatments for the main corporate wordmarks; reserve them for contextual social media campaigns and publication graphics.

## 4. Typography & Font System

The brand implements a clear, three-tiered typography system to balance raw visual power with narrative readability.

### 4.1 Ringold (Display Font)
* **Role & Usage:** Exclusively reserved for Major Titles, Large Headlines, and Impactful Short Phrases. It provides a punchy, vintage-vibe protest aesthetic.
* **Weight & Style:** Single option available: **"Sans"**. It must always be set in **ALL CAPS**.
* **Sizing Constraint:** Designed strictly for large-scale treatment. Use discretion when scaling, but never drop below a minimum size of `8pt`.

### 4.2 P22 Mackinac Pro (Editorial Serif)
* **Role & Usage:** Used for Editorial Headlines, Subheads, Continuous Body Copy, Block Quotes, and Captions. It injects a literary, human, and trusted tone into materials.
* **Weights Available:** `Medium`, `Medium Italic`, `Bold`, and `Bold Italic`.
* **Sizing Constraint:** Minimum size of `6pt`.

### 4.3 Inter (Clean Sans-Serif)
* **Role & Usage:** Used for Body Copy, Informational Data, Lists, Quotes, and Captions. It offers exceptional legible neutrality across digital devices.
* **Weights Available:** `Regular`, `Regular Italic`, `Bold`, and `Bold Italic`.
* **Sizing Constraint:** Minimum size of `6pt`.

### 4.4 Multi-Font Sentence Harmonization Formula
When mixing `Inter` and `P22 Mackinac Pro` dynamically within the exact same line or sentence structure (e.g., highlighting a specific phrase in a serif font amid sans-serif body copy), their optical heights must be perfectly equalized using the following exact mathematical formulas:

$$\text{pt size of P22 Mackinac Pro} = \frac{\text{pt size of Inter}}{96.7\%}$$

$$\text{pt size of Inter} = \text{pt size of P22 Mackinac Pro} \times 96.7\%$$

## 5. Typical Hierarchy & Layout Blueprint

When assembling layouts (such as social slides, posters, or reports), follow this structuring model:

1.  **HEADER (Display Tier):** Set in **Ringold (Sans)**, All Caps, heavy scaling. Used for statements like `GET INVOLVED` or `GO VOTE`.
2.  **SUBHEAD / CAPTION (Editorial Tier):** Set in **P22 Mackinac Pro (Bold or Medium Italic)** to anchor context.
3.  **BODY COPY (Informational Tier):** Set in **Inter (Regular)** at highly readable line heights, restricted to high-contrast color couplings (e.g., Dark text on a Lemon block background).
